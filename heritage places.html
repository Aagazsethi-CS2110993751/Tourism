World HERITAGE Sites In India


India takes pride in flaunting its unsurpassed heritage; eras over eras have influenced, moulded and face lifted the rich heritage of which we all are part of. Distinctive edifices, perennial culture and the determination to keep this incredibility have preserved for us an era no short of marvels. With a startling number of places and monuments enlisted in the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites, India has made an indelible mark in world history. The natural splendor maintaining its domain over many parts of the country boasts of magnificent wildlife heritage. With the shifts in ruling powers, India has become the thriving hub of multiple architectural marvels.




Taj Mahal

Taj mehal is one of the Seven Wonders of World.It is situated on the bank of river yamuna in the city of Agra. Taj Mahal is a monument of love.So it's a place worth visiting to memorize the love in your life.It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1631 as a tribute to his wife Mumtaz Mehal.



Qutub Minar

Located in the capital city,Qutub Minar is the tall architectural beauty that adorns Delhi with its magnificence.It is the second tallest Minar in the country.



Sun Temple

Also known as Black Pagoda,Sun temple is not only a notable architecture but is also one of the most important temples in south.It belongs to the 13th century and its unique shape of a gigantic chariot accentuates its importance as a heritage site.



Red Fort

The Red Fort is one of the most popular destinations in Delhi.It is situated along the Yamuna river,thw fort spreads over 254 acres,was designed to incorporate the pre-existing Salimgarh fort.



Ajanta Caves

As the unique representations of the Buddhist religion,31 rock-cut caves have been deemed as the world heritage site in the state of Maharashtra.These magnificient Buddhist caves belong to the 2nd century.



Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's tomb in Delhi is a heavenly building which remains as a magnificient rememberance of the Mughal Emperor Humayun.It is noted for its large number of notable Mughal architectures in Delhi which showsthe compositional wonderfulness of the Mughal dynasty.



Fatehpur Sikri

The city served as the capital of Mughal empire for 10 years.It is an architecture paradise,with most of the famous monuments of the city exhibiting the Timurid forms and styles of architecture.



Ellora Caves

The caves are famous for their sculptures strewn over all the 34 (12 Buddhist, 17 Hindu and 5 Jain) different caves temples. But it's shining glory is the Kailasa temple (cave 16); the world's largest monolithic sculpture, carved out by 7000 labourers over a period of 150 years.
